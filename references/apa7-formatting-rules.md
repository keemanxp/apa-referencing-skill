## 1. Book & eBook with DOI

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Ewert et al., 2014)
- Narrative: According to Ewert et al. (2014)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author, A. A., Author, B. B., & Author, C. C. (Year). Title of work in sentence case and italics. Publisher. https://doi.org/10.xxxx/xxxx
```

**Example:**
```
Ewert, E. W., Mitten, D. S., & Overholt, J. R. (2014). Natural environments and human health. CAB International. https://doi.org/10.1079/9781845939199.0000
```

**Notes:**
- Include the DOI using the format `https://doi.org/10.xxxx/xxxx`
- Book title is italicised and in sentence case.
- No place of publication needed in APA 7th Edition.

---

## 2. Book & eBook without DOI

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Foxall, 2018)
- Narrative: According to Foxall (2018)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of work in sentence case and italics. Publisher.
```

**Example:**
```
Foxall, G. R. (2018). Context and cognition in consumer psychology: How perception and emotion guide action. Routledge.
```

**Notes:**
- Do not include the database name or URL.
- In APA 7th Edition, the place of publication is no longer needed. Only the publisher's name is included.

## 3. Chapter in an Edited Book or eBook

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Aron et al., 2019)
- Narrative: Aron et al. (2019)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author, A. A., Author, B. B., & Author, C. C. (Year). Title of chapter in sentence case. In E. E. Editor, F. F. Editor, & G. G. Editor (Eds.), Title of book in sentence case and italics (pp. xx–xx). Publisher. https://doi.org/10.xxxx/xxxx
```

**Example:**
```
Aron, L., Botella, M., & Lubart, T. (2019). Culinary arts: Talent and their development. In R. F. Subotnik, P. Olszewski-Kubilius, & F. C. Worrell (Eds.), The psychology of high performance: Developing human potential into domain-specific talent (pp. 345–359). American Psychological Association. https://doi.org/10.1037/0000120-016
```

**Notes:**
- Use this format for both print and eBook edited book chapters, including from academic research databases.
- Include DOI if available.
- For the Editors, initials come FIRST (e.g. `In R. F. Subotnik`), unlike authors where surname comes first.
- The book title is italicised, not the chapter title.

## 4. Article with DOI from Research Databases

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Washington, 2014)
- Narrative: According to Washington (2014)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of article in sentence case. Journal Name in Title Case and Italics, Volume(Issue), Page–Page. https://doi.org/10.xxxx/xxxx
```

**Example:**
```
Washington, E. T. (2014). An overview of cyberbully in higher education. Adult Learning, 26(1), 21–27. https://doi.org/10.1177/1045159514558412
```

**Notes:**
- Italicise the journal name and the volume number.
- Do not italicise the issue number (in parentheses) or the article title.
- Always use articles from prominent research databases rather than random websites.

## 5. Article without DOI

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Moody, 2019)
- Narrative: According to Moody (2019)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of article in sentence case. Journal Name in Title Case and Italics, Volume(Issue), Page–Page.
```

**Example:**
```
Moody, M. S. (2019). If instructional coaching really works, why isn't it working? Educational Leadership, 77(3), 30–35.
```

**Notes:**
- No retrieval date is needed.
- Do not include database name or URL for articles from research databases that lack a DOI.

## 6. Open Access Journal Article without DOI

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Dayton, 2019)
- Narrative: According to Dayton (2019)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of article in sentence case. Journal Name in Title Case and Italics, Volume(Issue), Page–Page. URL
```

**Example:**
```
Dayton, K. J. (2019). Tangled arms: Modernizing and unifying the arm-of-the-state doctrine. The University of Chicago Law Review, 86(6), 1497–1737. https://bit.ly/2SkWwcy
```

**Notes:**
- Include the URL for open access articles without a DOI.
- A URL shortener (e.g. bit.ly, tinyurl.com) may be used if the URL is very long, but the link must be accessible and point directly to the article.

## 7. Conference Paper Presentation

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Mason & Missingham, 2019)
- Narrative: According to Mason and Missingham (2019)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year, Month Day–Day). Title of presentation in sentence case and italics [Type of presentation]. Conference Name, City, State/Country. URL
```

**Example:**
```
Mason, I., & Missingham, R. (2019, October 21–25). Research libraries, data curation, and workflows [Paper presentation]. eResearch Australasia Conference, Brisbane, QLD, Australia. https://bit.ly/2RGcFdn
```

**Notes:**
- Provide the full dates of the conference.
- Describe the presentation type in square brackets after the title. The description is flexible: `[Conference session]`, `[Paper presentation]`, `[Poster session]`, `[Keynote address]`.
- Include the conference name and location.
- Provide a link to the presentation materials (e.g. PDF, video) if available.

---

## 8. Conference Proceedings Published in a Journal

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Duckworth et al., 2019)
- Narrative: According to Duckworth et al. (2019)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author, A. A., Author, B. B., & Author, C. C. (Year). Title of article in sentence case. Proceedings Title in Title Case and Italics, Volume(Issue), Page–Page. https://doi.org/10.xxxx/xxxx
```

**Example:**
```
Duckworth, A. L., Quirk, A., Gallop, R., Hoyle, R. H., Kelly, D. R., & Matthews, M. D. (2019). Cognitive and noncognitive predictors of success. Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, USA, 116(47), 23499–23504. https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1910510116
```

**Notes:**
- If the journal indicates a proceedings title, use it. If not, cite as a regular journal article with DOI.

## 9. Conference Proceedings Published as a Book Chapter

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Bedenel et al., 2019)
- Narrative: According to Bedenel et al. (2019)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author, A. A., Author, B. B., & Author, C. C. (Year). Title of chapter in sentence case. In E. E. Editor, F. F. Editor, & G. G. Editor (Eds.), Series title: Vol. XX. Book title in sentence case and italics (pp. xx–xx). Publisher. https://doi.org/10.xxxx/xxxx
```

**Example:**
```
Bedenel, A.-L., Jourdan, L., & Biernacki, C. (2019). Probability estimation by an adapted genetic algorithm in web insurance. In R. Battiti, M. Brunato, I. Kotsireas, & P. Pardalos (Eds.), Lecture notes in computer science: Vol. 11353. Learning and intelligent optimization (pp. 225–240). Springer. https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-030-05348-2_21
```

**Notes:**
- Follows the chapter in an edited book format, with the inclusion of DOI.

## 10. Report by a Government Agency

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (National Cancer Institute, 2019)
- Narrative: According to National Cancer Institute (2019)

**Reference list template:**
```
Agency Name. (Year). Title of report in sentence case and italics (Publication No. xxx). Parent Agency. URL
```

**Example:**
```
National Cancer Institute. (2019). Taking time: Support for people with cancer (NIH Publication No. 18-2059). U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, National Institutes of Health. https://www.cancer.gov/publications/patient-education/takingtime.pdf
```

**Notes:**
- The specific agency responsible for the report appears as the author.
- Include the link to the report.
- If a publication number is given, include it in parentheses after the title.

## 11. White Paper by a Group Author

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Department for Business Innovation & Skills, 2016)
- Narrative: According to Department for Business Innovation & Skills (2016)

**Reference list template:**
```
Group Author. (Year). Title of white paper in sentence case and italics [White paper]. Publisher. URL
```

**Example:**
```
Department for Business Innovation & Skills. (2016). Success as a knowledge economy: Teaching excellent, social mobility and student choice [White paper]. Crown. https://www.gov.uk/government/uploads/system/uploads/attachment_data/file/523396/bis-16-265-success-as-a-knowledge-economy.pdf
```

**Notes:**
- Include the description `[White paper]` in square brackets after the title.

## 12. White Paper with Individual Authors

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Furst & DeMillo, 2006)
- Narrative: According to Furst and DeMillo (2006)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of white paper in sentence case and italics [White paper]. Publisher/Organisation. URL
```

**Example:**
```
Furst, M., & DeMillo, R. A. (2006). Creating symphonic-thinking computer science graduates for an increasingly competitive global environment [White paper]. Georgia Tech College of Computing. https://cis.temple.edu/~giorgio/threads_whitepaper.pdf
```

**Notes:**
- If individual authors are listed, format them like any other publication's author list.

## 13. Published Dissertation or Thesis

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Miranda, 2019)
- Narrative: According to Miranda (2019)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of dissertation or thesis in sentence case and italics (Publication No. xxxxx) [Doctoral dissertation/Master's thesis, Institution Name]. Database Name. URL
```

**Example:**
```
Miranda, C. (2019). Exploring the lived experiences of foster youth who obtained graduate level degrees: Self-efficacy, resilience, and the impact on identity development (Publication No. 27542827) [Doctoral dissertation, Pepperdine University]. PQDT Open. https://pqdtopen.proquest.com/doc/2309521814.html?FMT=AI
```

**Notes:**
- A dissertation or thesis is considered published when available from a database (e.g. ProQuest Dissertations and Theses Global, PQDT Open), an institutional repository, or an archive.
- If the database assigns a publication number, include it in parentheses after the title (not italicised).
- Include `[Doctoral dissertation, University Name]` or `[Master's thesis, University Name]` in square brackets.

---

## 14. Unpublished Dissertation or Thesis

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Harris, 2014)
- Narrative: According to Harris (2014)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of dissertation or thesis in sentence case and italics [Unpublished doctoral dissertation/Unpublished master's thesis]. Institution Name.
```

**Example:**
```
Harris, L. (2014). Instructional leadership perceptions and practices of elementary school leaders [Unpublished doctoral dissertation]. University of Virginia.
```

**Notes:**
- A dissertation or thesis is considered unpublished when accessed through direct reading at a specific library/repository but not publicly accessible online.
- If found online, use the published format instead.

## 15. Newspaper Article — Online

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Carey, 2019)
- Narrative: According to Carey (2019)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of article in sentence case. Newspaper Name in Title Case and Italics. URL
```

**Example:**
```
Carey, B. (2019, March 22). Can we get better at forgetting? The New York Times. https://www.nytimes.com/2019/03/22/health/memory-forgetting-psychology.html
```

**Notes:**
- The article must have an author and specific date.
- Only the newspaper name is italicised.

## 16. Newspaper Article — Print

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Harlan, 2013)
- Narrative: According to Harlan (2013)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of article in sentence case. Newspaper Name in Title Case and Italics, Pages.
```

**Example:**
```
Harlan, C. (2013, April 2). North Korea vows to restart shuttered nuclear reactor that can make bomb-grade plutonium. The Washington Post, A1, A4.
```

**Notes:**
- Only the newspaper name is italicised.
- Pages are listed as labelled in the newspaper (e.g. A1, A4).

## 17. Magazine Article — Online

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Schaefer & Shapiro, 2019)
- Narrative: According to Schaefer and Shapiro (2019)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year, Month Day). Title of article in sentence case. Magazine Name in Title Case and Italics, Volume(Issue), Page–Page. URL or DOI
```

**Example (with volume/issue):**
```
Schaefer, N. K., & Shapiro, B. (2019, September 6). New middle chapter in the story of human evolution. Science, 365(6457), 981–982. https://doi.org/10.1126/science.aay3550
```

**Example (without volume/issue):**
```
Schulman, M. (2019, September 9). Superfans: A love story. The New Yorker. https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2019/09/16/superfans-a-love-story
```

**Notes:**
- Online magazine articles follow a similar style to journal articles but with a specific date.
- If the magazine article does not have volume, issue, and/or page numbers, omit them.

## 19. Webpage (with date)

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Bologna, 2019)
- Narrative: According to Bologna (2019)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of page in sentence case and italics. Website Name. URL
```

**Example:**
```
Bologna, C. (2019, October 31). Why some people with anxiety love watching horror movies. HuffPost. https://www.huffpost.com/entry/anxiety-love-watching-horror-movies_l_5d277587e4b02a5a5d57b59e
```

**Notes:**
- Use webpages with clear indication of authors and date of publication.
- The name of the website (e.g. HuffPost) should be included before the URL.
- No retrieval date is needed when date of publication is given.

## 20. Webpage (no date)

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (U.S. Census Bureau, n.d.)
- Narrative: According to U.S. Census Bureau (n.d.)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author or Group. (n.d.). Title of page in sentence case and italics. Website Name. Retrieved Month Day, Year, from URL
```

**Example:**
```
U.S. Census Bureau. (n.d.). U.S. and world population clock. U.S. Department of Commerce. Retrieved January 9, 2020, from https://www.census.gov/popclock/
```

**Notes:**
- Use `(n.d.)` when no date is given.
- Since no date is given, include the retrieval date — the date when the page was accessed or the material was downloaded.

## 21. PowerPoint Slide or Lecture Note

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Jones, 2016)
- Narrative: According to Jones (2016)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of slides in sentence case and italics [PowerPoint slides]. Site Name. URL
```

**Example:**
```
Jones, J. (2016, March 23). Guided reading: Making the most of it [PowerPoint slides]. SlideShare. https://www.slideshare.net/hellojenjones/guided-reading-making-the-most-of-it
```

**Notes:**
- When slides are publicly available online, provide the site name and URL.
- The date refers to when the slides were uploaded (as shown on the website).

## 22. YouTube Video

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Asian Boss, 2020)
- Narrative: Asian Boss (2020)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author or Channel Name. (Year, Month Day). Title of video in sentence case and italics [Video]. YouTube. URL
```

**Example:**
```
Asian Boss. (2020, June 5). World's leading vaccine expert fact-checks COVID-19 vaccine conspiracy: Stay curious #22 [Video]. YouTube. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WQdLDMLrYIA
```

**Notes:**
- Use the name of the account that uploaded the video as the author. If the author uses their real name, follow the Surname, Initials convention.
- Include `[Video]` in square brackets after the title.

## 23. YouTube Channel

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (APA Publishing Training, n.d.)
- Narrative: APA Publishing Training (n.d.)

**Reference list template:**
```
Channel Name. (n.d.). Tab name [YouTube channel]. YouTube. Retrieved Month Day, Year, from URL
```

**Example:**
```
APA Publishing Training. (n.d.). Home [YouTube channel]. YouTube. Retrieved February 20, 2020, from https://www.youtube.com/user/PsycINFO/
```

**Notes:**
- YouTube channel pages begin on the "Home" tab by default. If citing a different tab (e.g. "Videos", "Playlists"), use that tab name instead of "Home".
- Include `[YouTube channel]` in square brackets.
- Provide a retrieval date because channel content changes over time and is not archived.

## 24. Film

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Fleming, 1939)
- Narrative: Fleming (1939)

**Reference list template:**
```
Director, A. A. (Director). (Year). Title of film in sentence case and italics [Film]. Production Company; Production Company.
```

**Example:**
```
Fleming, V. (Director). (1939). Gone with the wind [Film]. Selznick International Pictures; Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer.
```

**Notes:**
- Provide the director in the author element, followed by `(Director)`.
- Separate multiple production companies with a semicolon.

## 25. TV Series

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Serling, 1959–1964)
- Narrative: Serling (1959–1964)

**Reference list template:**
```
Producer, A. A. (Executive Producer). (Start Year–End Year). Title of series in sentence case and italics [TV series]. Production Company; Production Company.
```

**Example:**
```
Serling, R. (Executive Producer). (1959–1964). The twilight zone [TV series]. Cayuga Productions; CBS Productions.
```

**Notes:**
- Provide the executive producer(s) in the author element.
- Provide the year(s) during which the series aired.
- If the series is still airing, replace the end year with "present": `(2017–present)`.

## 26. Facebook Post

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (News from Science, 2019)
- Narrative: News from Science (2019)

**Reference list template:**
```
Account Name. (Year, Month Day). First 20 words of the post [Description of attached content] [Status update]. Facebook. URL
```

**Example:**
```
News From Science. (2019, June 21). Are you a fan of astronomy? Enjoy reading about what scientists have discovered in our solar system—and beyond? This [Image attached] [Status update]. Facebook. https://www.facebook.com/ScienceNOW/photos/a.117532185107/10156268057260108/?type=3&theater
```

**Notes:**
- Use the name associated with the account as the author.
- Provide the first 20 words of the post as the title. Count URLs, hashtags, and emojis as one word each.
- If a post includes images, videos, or shared content, indicate that in square brackets.
- Describe the post type (e.g. `[Status update]`, `[Video]`) in square brackets.

## 27. Instagram Photo or Video

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (Philadelphia Museum of Art, 2019)
- Narrative: Philadelphia Museum of Art (2019)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author Name [@handle]. (Year, Month Day). First 20 words of the caption in sentence case and italics [Photograph/Video]. Instagram. URL
```

**Example:**
```
Philadelphia Museum of Art [@philamuseum]. (2019, December 3). It's always wonderful to walk in and see my work in a collection where it's loved, and where people are [Photograph]. Instagram. https://www.instagram.com/p/B5oDnnNhOt4/
```

**Notes:**
- Provide the author name, then the handle (beginning with @) in square brackets.
- Provide the first 20 words of the caption as the title (italicised).
- Include a description of the post (e.g. `[Photograph]`, `[Video]`) in square brackets.
- The same format is used for TikTok videos.

## 28. Tweet

**In-text citation:**
- Parenthetical: (APA Databases, 2020)
- Narrative: APA Databases (2020)

**Reference list template:**
```
Author Name [@handle]. (Year, Month Day). First 20 words of the tweet in sentence case and italics [Description of attached content] [Tweet]. Twitter. URL
```

**Example:**
```
APA Databases [@APA_Databases]. (2020, September 2). We have curated a collection of journal articles from APA PsycArticles on #COVID19. View: https://bit.ly/2QrWj76 for recent coverage [Image attached] [Tweet]. Twitter. https://twitter.com/APA_Databases/status/1301175576703664128
```

**Notes:**
- Provide the author name, then the Twitter handle (beginning with @) in square brackets.
- Provide the first 20 words of the tweet as the title. Count URLs, hashtags, and emojis as one word each.
- If the tweet includes an image, video, poll, or thumbnail, indicate that in brackets: `[Image attached]`, `[Video attached]`, `[Thumbnail with link attached]`.
- Include `[Tweet]` in square brackets after the title.

## Quick In-Text Citation Rules Summary

| Number of Authors | Parenthetical | Narrative |
|---|---|---|
| 1 author | (Smith, 2020) | Smith (2020) or According to Smith (2020) |
| 2 authors | (Smith & Jones, 2020) | Smith and Jones (2020) |
| 3+ authors | (Smith et al., 2020) | Smith et al. (2020) |
| Group author (first use) | (World Health Organization [WHO], 2020) | World Health Organization (WHO, 2020) |
| Group author (subsequent) | (WHO, 2020) | WHO (2020) |
| No author | ("Title of Work," 2020) | "Title of Work" (2020) |
| No date | (Smith, n.d.) | Smith (n.d.) |
